> **Tagline:** Turn hotspots into actionable intel.

`DV_HeatMap` paints density, intensity, or incident data across any map texture with cinematic clarity. Feed it world-space coordinates, let it handle autoscaling, gradients, and hit testing, and you’ll deliver rich spatial insights that feel tailor-made for your world.

## Why it captivates

- **Spatially aware.** `set_world_region(origin, size)` keeps every point perfectly aligned to your map, whether you’re visualizing a tactical floorplan or a sprawling overworld.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L9-L70】【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L144-L170】
- **Adaptive intensity.** Flip `set_autoscale(true)` to let the control normalize incoming data, or pin ranges with `set_intensity_range(min, max)` for consistent comparisons across views.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L108-L143】
- **Ready for interaction.** `point_hovered` and `point_clicked` expose indices, labels, positions, and intensity so you can trigger tooltips or drill-downs with ease.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L188-L216】

## Ship it fast

1. **Pick your backdrop** – `set_background_texture(texture)` and choose how it fits with `set_texture_fit_mode(DV_HeatMap.FIT_*)`.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L144-L170】【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L171-L187】
2. **Map the world** – `set_world_region(origin, size)` describes the playable area so every incident lands exactly where it happened.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L171-L187】
3. **Stream events** – call `add_point(event_type, tooltip, world_position, intensity)` as data arrives. Need a refresh? `clear_points()` wipes the slate.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L72-L120】
4. **Tailor the look** – craft cinematic palettes with `set_gradient(colors, stops)` and control falloff via `set_point_radius(px)`.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L108-L170】

## API highlights

- `set_points(positions: PackedVector2Array, intensities: PackedFloat32Array)` – batch updates for lightning-fast refreshes.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L132-L143】
- `get_point_positions()` / `get_point_intensities()` – sync other widgets or analytics layers with the heat map’s current state.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L120-L132】
- `set_point_radius(px: float)` – tune the kernel to match the scale of your data.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L176-L187】
- `set_intensity_range(min: float, max: float)` – compare two different regions with identical scaling for credible insights.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L108-L127】

## Integrates seamlessly

Even though `DV_HeatMap` is a custom control, it slots into the same themed dashboards as every DVComponent thanks to shared palette seeds, typography, and animation timing delivered by `DVTheme`. Pair it with detail panels or charts and you’ll have a unified command center players and producers can’t stop exploring.【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DV_HeatMap.xml†L5-L70】【F:docs/toolkits/godot-dataviz/doc_classes/DVTheme.xml†L5-L86】

HeatMapView is your shortcut to the kind of spatial analytics usually reserved for AAA studios—now it’s just another node in your scene tree.
